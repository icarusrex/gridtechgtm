Our thinking

Five things we believe about grid GTM.

01

Utilities don't buy technology. They buy regulatory cover.

Every pitch deck that leads with architecture has lost before it started. Lead with the regulatory, tariff or operational pressure your buyer is accountable for. The tech is the answer, not the opening.

02

Conferences aren't marketing. They're the pipeline.

One well-run meeting at Enlit is worth a quarter of cold outreach. We treat events as a pre, during, and post pipeline system. Not a booth, a badge and a hope that something sticks.

03

POCs die in procurement, not engineering.

You won the trial. You shipped the integration. Then it spent eleven months on a purchasing director's desk. The fix isn't better tech. It's a commercial motion running in parallel from week one.

04

Consortiums are moats. Most founders miss the window.

Mercury, EPRI, NREL, CEN/CLC, SMARTEN workstreams. These are where standards, roadmaps and the next three years of procurement language get set. Getting in is a strategy, not a favour.

05

Activity isn't motion.

Dials, MQLs, impressions, a CRM full of rows. None of it means pipeline if the machine isn't engineered. We treat GTM as infrastructure. Every stage has a signal, an owner, a fallback.

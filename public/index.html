The problem

Grid tech doesn't sell like SaaS.

Demand is created by regulation, not marketing. The buyer is a risk-averse engineer with a procurement department behind them. And the sales cycle outlives most sales hires. Most grid software companies respond with a generalist playbook — and stall at the pilot. We turn the constraints of utility procurement into an operating system for territory revenue.

Our process

The Territory Operating System

A bid-writer's discipline applied to a whole territory: measurable checkpoints, board-ready reporting, and a pipeline your CFO can audit.

Day 0 Territory Charter Kick-Off ICP, market thesis and win criteria on one page. Which regulatory catalysts, which buyer types, which protocols in scope.
Day 14 Signal Map & Account Prioritisation Every DSO, CPO and aggregator in the territory ranked by regulatory trigger, tender history and technical fit — not by employee count.
Day 30 First Utility Conversations Warm entry through standards bodies, industry events and the operator network — not cold sequences that utilities never answer.
Day 90 Qualified Pipeline & First Bids Scoped technical proposals in flight. Weekly growth loop with delivery so what sales promises, engineering can ship.
Day 180 RFPs Submitted, Consortium Built The bids that matter answered on spec and on deadline — with the local partners and consortium structure to be credible.
Day 365 Closed, Referenceable Revenue Closed inside the cycle, not after it: the funded route — grants, consortiums, regulator sandboxes — beats the 36-month RFP clock. It is how the £700K Community DSO deal landed, and it produces the reference that opens the next market.
